ABOUT US

Inspired by Jean Henry Dunant's humanitarian efforts during the Battle of Solferino in 1859, the Young Red Cross (YRC) aims to be a beacon of compassion and service. Dunant, deeply moved by the suffering of wounded soldiers, envisioned a neutral organization to provide aid to those in need. His vision led to the creation of the Red Cross in 1864, a symbol of global relief. At Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), the YRC chapter was established in 2002 to extend this mission on campus. Over the years, we've strived to embody the values of humanity, impartiality, neutrality, and volunteerism.

"Humanity is not a privilege, it is a responsibility."

- Jean Henry Dunant
